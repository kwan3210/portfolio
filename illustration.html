Queensland Youth Week Art Competition | 2020

Teenagers and young adults today often separate people into categories by their age, appearance, gender, school (Private / public), University (UQ / QUT), fashion (e-boys / VSCO girl etc..), ethnicity, cultural background and body shape (big / skinny). It can be difficult for us to understand each other because we all have different values and beliefs. For example, a 14-year-old Chinese girl Jennie, who goes to public high school may not fully understand a 17-year-old Australian boy Tom, who goes to private high school.

However, even though we are different, we are the in the category of YOUTH. And we cannot deny the fact that the existence of each other empowers the youth and community. Even though we don’t fully understand each other, we feel empowered just by standing up together. Young people have so many potentials and energy that can bring positive impacts to the society.

In my artwork, I tried to represent how young individuals unite together and empower other youth living in Queensland to connect and grow together. Regardless of how they look and dress, they are being connected by painting arts and putting posters on the wall for empowering our youth to be future leaders in Queensland. Once we are all connected, we can make changes together and let our voice be heard for issues like climate change and equal education.
